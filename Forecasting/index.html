Predictive Analytics for Cash Management

I. Background

The following study leverages experimental cash flow data from multiple ATM sites operated by a private banking institution throughout the United States. Advanced stochastic forecasting methodologies were employed to enhance cash logistics optimization, yielding quantifiable improvements in both cost reduction and operational efficiency. For data protection purposes, actual location identifiers have been changed.

II. Business Problem

Financial institutions face significant challenges in cash management:

  • Inefficient cash distribution leading to high third-party logistics costs
  • Suboptimal ATM cash levels causing service disruptions
  • Manual forecasting processes prone to errors and delays
  • Lack of data-driven insights for strategic cash flow decisions

The core objective of this study was to predict cash requirements accurately and optimize replenishment decisions, minimizing operational costs while maintaining service levels.

III. Forecasting Modeling

To address the problem, the study analyzed historical transaction data while incorporating:

  • Stochastic Forecasting Models: Implemented Monte Carlo simulations for uncertainty quantification
  • Time Series Analysis: Autoregressive models, seasonal decomposition, and trend analysis
  • Machine Learning Pipeline: Automated feature engineering and model retraining
  • Optimization Algorithms: Cost-minimization functions for cash allocation

This approach enabled data-driven, location-specific forecasting and operational recommendations without exposing sensitive or proprietary data.
